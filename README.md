# Atlas

Atlas is a student discovery platform built to make campus life easier to navigate. At universities like NTNU, student events, organizations, and opportunities are often scattered across Instagram pages, posters, websites, and word of mouth. Atlas brings this information into one place, making it easier for students to discover what is happening and get involved.

## The Problem

Student life is highly fragmented. Important information is spread across many channels, which creates unnecessary friction for students trying to engage with campus life.

This leads to several problems:

- Students miss relevant events and opportunities simply because they never see them
- Student organizations struggle to reach the right audience
- New and international students face a particularly high barrier to discovering communities
- There is no clear, centralized overview of what exists and what is happening

At NTNU, this means that discovering student life often depends too much on luck, social circles, or constant social media checking.

## The Solution

Atlas solves this by creating a centralized platform where students can browse events and organizations through one clean and structured interface.

The MVP focuses on:

- Discovering student events
- Browsing student organizations
- Viewing key information such as category, date, location, and description
- Making campus life easier to explore from one place instead of many disconnected channels

The longer-term vision is for Atlas to become a digital entry point to student life, where students can not only discover events and organizations, but also access opportunities, updates, and useful student tools.

## Why It Matters

Atlas is not just a technical project. It is a product built around a real user problem: discovery.

Better access to student life can lead to:

- stronger student engagement
- better social belonging
- increased participation in organizations
- greater awareness of academic, social, and career opportunities
- lower barriers for first-year and international students

For student organizations, Atlas can also become a better distribution channel for visibility and attendance.

## Target Market

Atlas is initially built for NTNU students, with particular relevance for:

- first-year students
- students who want to get more involved in campus life
- exchange and international students
- student organizations that want better visibility

The problem Atlas addresses is not unique to NTNU. Most universities face similar fragmentation, which gives the concept broader long-term potential.

## Early Traction

Atlas is still at an early stage, but the project already reflects real product development work:

- identified a concrete user problem based on student experience
- scoped a focused MVP instead of overbuilding
- built and deployed a working web product
- defined a clear initial launch context in the NTNU student environment
- designed the concept with future scalability in mind

## Product Approach

Atlas has been developed with an MVP mindset:

- start narrow
- solve one clear problem well
- validate usefulness before expanding functionality

Instead of building a large platform immediately, the first version focuses on a clean frontend experience for browsing events and organizations. This makes the product faster to build, easier to test, and more flexible to improve over time.
